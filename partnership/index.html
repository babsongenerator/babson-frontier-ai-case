Partnership

Three clean ways to bring frontier AI to Babson entrepreneurs.

Each tier is scoped — start small, scale on success. Each one can run with one frontier lab or across all three. The figures below use the published per-user pricing from spring 2026; vendor-quoted institutional discounts will move the totals in Babson's favor.

Three tiers

From a single course to a campus-wide partnership.

Tier 1

Pilot

One course, one semester. ~30 students. Seat licenses with one lab + a faculty workshop.

Indicative cost: ~$5–10k per semester (seat licenses at $15–20/user/month for 4 months, plus a workshop).

A clean way to start. Low risk, high learning.

Tier 2

Department

Entrepreneurship division, full academic year. ~600 students, all entrepreneurship faculty. Pooled seat licenses, classroom integration, and a faculty PD cohort.

Indicative cost: ~$120–150k / year (600 students × $15–20/month × 12 months, before institutional discount).

A natural extension of what already works at Babson.

Tier 3

Institution

Campus-wide, all programs. Named-partner status with one or more frontier labs, research compute, and a flagship student-builder program funded jointly with donors.

Indicative cost: ~$350–500k / year (institutional volume pricing, multi-lab arrangement).

A defining commitment, sized to Babson's reputation.

Co-funding

The external environment is unusually friendly.

Two outside sources of capital meaningfully reduce the net cost of any of the tiers above:

  • The Lilly Endowment AI in Higher Education initiative — $500M total, with implementation grants up to $25M per institution.
  • The U.S. Department of Education's May 2026 priority on advancing AI in education, which will shape federal discretionary funding across multiple programs.

Donors funding the Build Fund and G1000 have also signaled real interest in supporting AI-era entrepreneurship specifically.

A practical first step

The Pilot is designed to be easy to say yes to.

A single semester, a single course, one frontier lab. The cost lands well inside discretionary range. The learning lands well inside the next budget cycle. If the Pilot works — and the peer evidence suggests it will — the Department tier is a natural next move, with a clean story to take to donors who already fund Babson entrepreneurship.

This is not a leap. It is the next step on a path Babson has been walking for thirty years, and that "AI in Action" has already started extending.
