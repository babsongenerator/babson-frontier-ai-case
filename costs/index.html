What it costs

The cost of frontier-AI tooling in May 2026.

Per-user pricing for institutional access has come down meaningfully over the past eighteen months. Below: the published rates from each frontier lab as of May 2026.

Published pricing · May 2026

Per-user rates, three labs.

Provider Plan Per user / month Notes
OpenAI ChatGPT Business $20.00 (committed) Reduced by $5 in April 20261.
Google AI Pro for Education Education tier · via reseller Education-specific tier available to Workspace for Education customers; pricing handled through Google's reseller channel2.
Anthropic Claude for Education Negotiated Institutional contracts; includes Learning Mode for student reasoning3.

These are list rates for the standard institutional tiers. Education-specific discounts and volume pricing typically move the totals further in an institution's favor.

What that means in practice

The unit economics have changed.

Institutional access to frontier AI is now priced at the same order of magnitude as a standard productivity tool. The OpenAI rate above is comparable to widely-deployed enterprise SaaS — and falls well below the per-user cost of most other institutional software packages in higher education. Anthropic's negotiated pricing for education tends to land in the same neighborhood, depending on scale3.

The trajectory matters too: OpenAI's April 2026 price cut1 followed a year of similar reductions across the field. Whatever an institution chooses to do, the underlying tools are now cheaper than they were eighteen months ago, and the direction is downward.

Beyond per-user pricing

The non-seat layer.

Per-user seat licensing is one of three cost layers an institutional partnership typically involves. The other two: API and token consumption for student-built applications (priced per million tokens at each lab) and faculty professional-development investment to translate access into curriculum. Those numbers are highly use-case-dependent; the seat-pricing rates above are the most transferable comparison.
