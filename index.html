A note for President Spinelli

Babson built modern entrepreneurship education. Frontier AI is the next chapter — and Babson is built to lead it.

This is a short brief, by design. The case is simple: the tools that define the next decade of building are arriving now, and they fit Babson's model — action, applied, founder-first — better than they fit almost any other institution in the country.

The Babson legacy

A track record very few institutions can claim.

For more than three decades Babson has been recognized as the leader in entrepreneurship education — the only American business school to make entrepreneurship the center of the curriculum, not an elective on the side. The F.W. Olin Graduate School, the Babson Build Fund (roughly 500 students backed each year), and the G1000 initiative are not marketing language; they are infrastructure. Babson graduates start companies. Babson alumni run them at scale.

That foundation is exactly why this next move is natural. The question is not whether Babson can lead in AI-era entrepreneurship — it is whether Babson chooses to do so on its own timeline.

The moment

Three labs are defining the tools founders will use.

Frontier AI is, today, the work of three companies: Google, Anthropic, and OpenAI. Everything else in the consumer AI conversation — Copilot, Perplexity, the long tail of vertical products — is built on top of these three. Founders working at the frontier are concentrating their tool use accordingly.

The labs themselves are interested in higher education. Each has launched institutional access programs over the past eighteen months. The doors are open; the cost is approachable. The opening this presents to Babson — to be among the first business-focused institutions to formally partner with all three — will not stay open indefinitely.

Why Babson is built for this

The fit is unusually good.

The traits these tools reward — fast iteration, comfort with ambiguity, willingness to ship, judgment over recipe — are exactly the traits Babson has spent decades cultivating in its students. Frontier AI does not change what Babson teaches. It compresses the time between a student's idea and the first version they can put in front of a customer, sometimes from a semester to a weekend.

For a school whose graduates are measured by what they actually build, that compression is the entire game.
