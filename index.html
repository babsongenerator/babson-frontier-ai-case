A note for President Spinelli

Babson built modern entrepreneurship education. Frontier AI is the next chapter — and Babson is already moving.

The brief that follows is short by design, and informational rather than a recommendation. It lays out four things: Babson's track record and what's already in motion, what student entrepreneurs are doing in the field, what mature AI partnerships look like at peer institutions, and what the underlying frontier-model tools cost.

The Babson legacy

A track record very few institutions can claim.

Babson has been ranked the #1 MBA program for entrepreneurship by U.S. News & World Report for 32 consecutive years1. The F.W. Olin Graduate School and the Summer Venture Program2, along with a broader ecosystem of entrepreneurship education programs and pitch competitions, back hundreds of student ventures each year.

That foundation is exactly why the next chapter — frontier AI as the operating environment for the next generation of founders — is one Babson is unusually well-positioned to write.

Already in motion

Babson + Founderz: the literacy layer is in place.

In February 2026 Babson launched "AI in Action", a certificate program built with Founderz, introducing the AI-THINK methodology — a structured framework for applying frontier AI tools in entrepreneurial settings3. President Spinelli framed the move directly: "For today's professionals, staying competitive means understanding how to apply AI thoughtfully and responsibly across every area of business."3

The moment

Three labs are building the tools founders use.

Frontier AI in 2026 is, in practice, the work of three companies: Anthropic, Google, and OpenAI. Each is shipping enterprise-grade tools that go far beyond chat. The three differ in useful ways.

Anthropic's Claude — with Opus 4.7 as the current flagship, released in April 20264 — is widely treated as the strongest "architect" model: careful, literal, well-suited to high-stakes reasoning. Anthropic's Claude for Education program ships a Learning Mode designed to guide student reasoning rather than simply produce answers5.

OpenAI's GPT-5.5, released in April 20266, leads on agentic execution. Workspace Agents — launched the same week — let students run multi-step workflows directly inside enterprise tools like Salesforce, Slack, and HubSpot7.

Google's Gemini 3.1 Pro8 brings native multimodality (video, image, audio, and text in a single pass) and a one-million-token context window. Google Workspace Intelligence, also launched in April 2026, weaves Gemini directly into the Google Workspace suite many students already use daily9.

Why Babson is built for this

The fit is unusually good.

The traits these tools reward — fast iteration, comfort with ambiguity, willingness to ship, judgment over recipe — are exactly the traits Babson has spent decades cultivating in its students. Frontier AI does not change what Babson teaches. It compresses the time between a student's idea and the first version they can put in front of a customer.

For a school whose graduates are measured by what they actually build, that compression is the entire game.
