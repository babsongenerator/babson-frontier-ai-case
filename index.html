The three providers

Three companies, three distinct strengths.

2026's AI landscape is, in practice, the work of three companies. Each ships enterprise-grade education tools today. None is interchangeable; each fits a different kind of student work.

Adoption patterns at peer institutions trend toward all three — different providers for different tasks — rather than picking a single winner.

Provider 1 of 3

OpenAI

Strength: agentic execution. Flagship: GPT-5.5 (April 2026)1. Workspace Agents run multi-step workflows inside Salesforce, Slack, HubSpot2.

Anthropic

Strength: careful reasoning, pedagogy. Flagship: Claude Opus 4.73. Claude for Education ships Learning Mode — guides student reasoning rather than just produces answers4.

Provider 3 of 3

Google

Strength: multimodality + Workspace. Flagship: Gemini 3.1 Pro5. 1M-token context, native video, plus Workspace Intelligence6. Already at 1,000+ institutions7.

Peer adoption

Where each provider has been deployed.

Different institutional shapes — universal MBA seats, single-lab campus-wide deals, system-wide rollouts — but each of the three providers has at least one elite peer institution deploying it at scale.

OpenAI · 2024–25

Wharton, Oxford, UCLA, CSU.

Wharton put ChatGPT in every MBA student's hands8; Oxford rolled out to all staff and students9; CSU reached 460,000+ students10.

Anthropic · 2025

Northeastern, LSE, Champlain.

Northeastern signed campus-wide across 13 campuses11; LSE made enterprise Claude free for all students; Champlain — a small private peer — got two years of free Claude Pro12.

Google · 2025–26

Rice, Houston, 1,000+ others.

Rice deployed Gemini campus-wide13; Houston rolled out with data sovereignty14; Google's published footprint reaches 10M+ U.S. students7.

Three things to note

Three things that are true of all three providers today.

These are descriptive observations, not arguments. Each is sourced.

Mature

Education-tier products ship today.

Each provider offers an institution-grade product with SSO, Workspace/Office integration, and data-isolation guarantees standard.

Priced

Inside routine software budgets.

Per-user pricing now sits well inside what institutions spend on routine software. ChatGPT Business: $20/user/month15.

Deployed

Already in peer institutions.

1,000+ higher-ed institutions on Gemini7; campus-wide Anthropic deals at Northeastern, LSE, Champlain1112; ChatGPT Edu at Oxford and CSU910.
