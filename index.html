A note for President Spinelli

Babson built modern entrepreneurship education. Institutional AI access is the next chapter — and Babson is already moving.

Three companies — OpenAI, Anthropic, and Google — lead AI in 2026. Bringing all three to Babson is affordable, low-friction, and benefits everyone on campus — and it sits squarely inside Babson's identity as the global leader in entrepreneurship education.

The Babson legacy

A track record very few institutions can claim.

Babson has been ranked the #1 MBA program for entrepreneurship by U.S. News & World Report for 32 consecutive years1. The F.W. Olin Graduate School and the Summer Venture Program2, alongside a broader ecosystem of entrepreneurship programs and pitch competitions, back hundreds of student ventures each year.

The traits these tools reward — fast iteration, comfort with ambiguity, willingness to ship, judgment over recipe — are the traits Babson has spent decades cultivating.

Already in motion

Babson + Founderz: the literacy layer is in place.

In February 2026, Babson launched "AI in Action" with Founderz, introducing the AI-THINK methodology — a structured framework for applying AI tools in entrepreneurial settings3.

"For today's professionals, staying competitive means understanding how to apply AI thoughtfully and responsibly across every area of business."
— President Spinelli3

The three providers

Three companies, three distinct strengths.

2026's AI landscape is, in practice, the work of three companies. Each ships enterprise-grade tools that go beyond chat. None is interchangeable; each fits a different kind of work.

The shape of the move

Easy, affordable, common-sense.

The case rests on three things that all three providers make true today.

Easy

Integrates with what Babson already runs.

Workspace, Office, SSO. Education-tier plans exist for exactly this kind of institutional rollout.

Affordable
