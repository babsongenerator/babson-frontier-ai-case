A note for President Spinelli

Babson built modern entrepreneurship education. Institutional AI access is the next chapter — and Babson is already moving.

Three companies — OpenAI, Anthropic, and Google — are the leading AI providers in 2026. The case this brief lays out is straightforward: bringing all three to Babson is affordable, low-friction, and benefits everyone on campus — and the move sits squarely inside Babson's identity as the global leader in entrepreneurship education.

Value for everyone → The three providers
The Babson legacy

A track record very few institutions can claim.

Babson has been ranked the #1 MBA program for entrepreneurship by U.S. News & World Report for 32 consecutive years1. The F.W. Olin Graduate School and the Summer Venture Program2, alongside a broader ecosystem of entrepreneurship education programs and pitch competitions, back hundreds of student ventures each year.

That foundation is exactly what makes the next chapter — institutional AI access as standard infrastructure — a natural fit. The traits these tools reward are the traits Babson has spent decades cultivating: fast iteration, comfort with ambiguity, willingness to ship, judgment over recipe.

Already in motion

Babson + Founderz: the literacy layer is in place.

In February 2026, Babson launched "AI in Action", a certificate program built with Founderz that introduces the AI-THINK methodology — a structured framework for applying AI tools in entrepreneurial settings3. President Spinelli framed the move directly: "For today's professionals, staying competitive means understanding how to apply AI thoughtfully and responsibly across every area of business."3

The literacy layer is in place. The three providers are the tools the literacy is for.

The three providers

Three companies, three distinct strengths.

The 2026 AI landscape is, in practice, the work of three companies. Each ships enterprise-grade tools that go far beyond chat — and each is differentiated in useful ways.

OpenAI is the most widely-deployed and the strongest on agentic execution. GPT-5.5, released in April 20264, leads on multi-step workflow automation. Workspace Agents — launched the same week — let students run agents that operate inside Salesforce, Slack, and HubSpot on their behalf5.

Anthropic is widely treated as the "architect" of the three — careful, literal, well-suited to high-stakes reasoning. Claude Opus 4.7 is the current flagship6. Claude for Education ships a Learning Mode designed to guide student reasoning rather than just produce answers — pedagogy first7.

Google brings native multimodality and a one-million-token context window with Gemini 3.1 Pro8, plus deep integration into the Google Workspace tools many students already use daily — Workspace Intelligence launched in April 20269. Google has also placed Gemini for Education in 1,000+ higher-education institutions reaching more than 10 million students10.

A relationship with all three — or with one to start — does not require choosing a winner. It puts Babson students alongside whichever provider fits the work they're doing that week.

Why this is straightforward

Easy, affordable, common-sense.

Easy: these tools integrate with the Workspace, Office, and SSO infrastructure Babson already runs. The three providers offer education-tier plans built around exactly this kind of institutional rollout.

Affordable: per-user pricing has come down significantly over the past eighteen months. The combined cost for institutional access is materially less than the marginal cost of most other software in higher education — and meaningfully less than a single endowed faculty line.

Common-sense: it benefits students, faculty, and staff — and reinforces what Babson is already known for. Entrepreneurship leaders are the populations who benefit most from these tools. Babson putting all three providers in their hands is consistent with who Babson is.
