The primary brief stands on its own; this is the layer beneath it.

A.1

On Microsoft Copilot

Microsoft Copilot is Babson's institutional Microsoft 365 layer and a daily tool for many faculty and staff. The three providers discussed in the primary brief are the upstream model substrates that Copilot itself draws on — and that students reach directly when their projects outgrow Copilot's surface. The two layers are complementary, not competing.

A.1.1 What Copilot is, in Microsoft's own words

Microsoft's official documentation describes Copilot Chat as “powered by the latest models,” explicitly naming GPT-5 (and earlier GPT-4 generations) as the engines behind both the standard and priority tiersA1. Microsoft's own anniversary blog credits OpenAI's GPT-4 Turbo and DALL-E 3 as the models powering Copilot's generation, search, and vision featuresA2. Microsoft and OpenAI's published partnership terms run through 2032A3.

In November 2025, Microsoft, NVIDIA, and Anthropic announced a complementary strategic partnership. Microsoft committed up to $5B and NVIDIA up to $10B to Anthropic; in return, Anthropic's Claude family is available on Microsoft Foundry and runs inside the Copilot product family alongside OpenAI's modelsA4A5.

Why both layers matter. Microsoft itself routes both OpenAI and Anthropic models through Copilot. The model-substrate layer — where pricing, terms, and product direction are set — is OpenAI, Anthropic, and Google. Copilot is the surface where most staff work today; direct provider access is the surface students reach when their work goes beyond it. Both belong in Babson's stack.

A.1.2 Copilot in higher education

Microsoft does sell a higher-ed Copilot product. The canonical landing page is microsoft.com/education/products/copilot-in-educationA6. Copilot Chat is free under Microsoft 365 A1/A3/A5 licenses; the Microsoft 365 Copilot add-on is $18/user/month for academic customers (vs. $30 commercial).

InstitutionDeploymentNotes
University of South Carolina Campus deployment of Microsoft 365 Copilot 84% of users (faculty, students, admin, IT, procurement) reported saving 1–5 hours/weekA7.
University of Manchester 300-user pilot across academic and back-office cohorts Microsoft customer story emphasizing creative thinking and continuous learning use casesA8.

The volume of higher-ed Copilot deployments is meaningfully smaller than the foundation-provider equivalents documented in the primary brief: 1,000+ institutions on Gemini for Education10, ChatGPT Edu at the CSU system's 500K+ users14, campus-wide Anthropic deals at Northeastern (13 global campuses)16, LSE17, and Champlain18.

A.1.3 Implication for institutional strategy

Copilot is the right surface for staff and faculty workflows that already happen inside Outlook, Word, Excel, and Teams — precisely because those workflows live in Microsoft 365 today, and Copilot meets them where they are. Direct provider access (OpenAI, Anthropic, Google) addresses a different need: the student building a venture, the faculty member running a long-context literature review, the classroom assistant that has to use Anthropic's Learning Mode specifically. Almost every peer institution running Copilot for staff productivity has also pursued a direct relationship with at least one of the three foundation providers for student-facing and academic use. The two complement each other; neither replaces the other.

A.2.2 The CSU — OpenAI deal, unpacked

California State University's contract with OpenAI is the largest publicly-disclosed deal between a peer institution and any of the three providers15. The structure is informative:

PhaseUsers coveredWindowCost
Initial deployment~40,000January – June 2025$1.9M
Full systemwide rollout500,000+July 2025 – June 2026$15M
Total—18 months$17M

The CSU deal reflects ChatGPT Edu volume pricing at a scale roughly 100x Babson's. Per-user economics at smaller institutional scale skew toward list rather than CSU's negotiated rate. Both LAist and CalMatters reported the $17M figure on May 1, 202615.

A.2.3 Indicative Babson-scale math

For an institution of Babson's size (roughly 5,000 active student, faculty, and staff users), list pricing implies the following annual envelopes:

ProviderList rate × 5,000 users × 12 monthsList annual
OpenAI (ChatGPT Business)$20 × 5,000 × 12$1.20M
Google (AI Pro for Education)$15 × 5,000 × 12$0.90M
Anthropic (Claude for Education)Negotiated — assumed comparable~$0.9–1.2M

Education-tier discounts, volume pricing, and bundled enterprise agreements typically reduce these list-rate totals. A multi-provider arrangement with assignment-by-use-case (rather than universal seats with each provider) further reduces aggregate cost. The exact figures depend on procurement; the order of magnitude is firm.

A.2.4 What is not publicly disclosed

Several institutional deal totals have not been published:

  • Northeastern University × Anthropic: 13-global-campus deal; dollar amount undisclosed16.
  • University of Oxford × OpenAI: five-year partnership announced September 2025; Oxford-specific dollar amount undisclosed (the broader OpenAI “NextGenAI” consortium totals $50M across ~15 institutions)12.
  • Wharton × OpenAI: May 2024 strategic AI investment; institution-wide dollar amount undisclosed11.
  • LSE, Champlain, Rice, Houston: contracts described publicly; dollar amounts undisclosed17181920.
  • Per-school Gemini for Education deal totals: Google publishes the aggregate (1,000+ institutions, 10M+ U.S. students) but not single-school amounts10.
A.3

Glossary

Provider-specific terms used in the primary brief, briefly defined.

TermProviderWhat it means
GPT-5.5 OpenAI OpenAI's current flagship model family (released April 2026)1. Successor to GPT-5 and GPT-4 generations. Underlies ChatGPT Business, ChatGPT Edu, and Microsoft Copilot.
Workspace Agents OpenAI OpenAI product launched April 2026 that runs multi-step automated workflows inside enterprise tools (Salesforce, Slack, HubSpot, etc.)2. The agent layer sitting on top of GPT-5.5.
Operator OpenAI Underlying browser-automation engine inside Workspace Agents. Navigates web pages, fills forms, completes purchases, and similar.
Claude Opus 4.7 Anthropic Anthropic's flagship reasoning model (released April 2026)4. Designed for high-stakes reasoning with careful, literal adherence to instructions. The model behind Claude Enterprise and Claude for Education's Architect tier.
Learning Mode Anthropic Pedagogically-designed mode in Claude for Education5. Instead of returning direct answers, it guides students through reasoning steps — closer to how faculty teach than to how chatbots typically respond.
Gemini 3.1 Pro Google Google's current flagship model7. Native multimodal handling of text, image, audio, and video in a single pass. 1-million-token context window.
Workspace Intelligence Google Google product launched April 2026 that weaves Gemini directly into the Google Workspace suite for agentic work8. The Google equivalent of OpenAI's Workspace Agents.
1M-token context Google & Anthropic A model's “context window” is the maximum amount of text it can read at one time. 1-million-token capacity (Opus 4.7 and Gemini 3.1 Pro) is roughly 750,000 words — enough to ingest a hundred academic papers or a textbook in a single pass.
ChatGPT Edu OpenAI Education-specific version of ChatGPT Enterprise3. Includes SSO, data-isolation guarantees (no training on customer prompts), and institutional admin controls.
