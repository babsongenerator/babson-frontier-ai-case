Outcomes

What changes when these tools are in students' hands.

Frontier AI does not replace the entrepreneur. It collapses the distance between an idea and the first version a customer can hold. The Build Fund, G1000, and the Summer Venture Program give Babson students runway; these tools give them velocity. A few 2026 SVP teams are already showing what that looks like.

Babson · Summer Venture Program 2026

Four teams worth looking at.

Each of these ventures is in the 2026 SVP cohort. Each is using frontier AI tooling today — generally through personal subscriptions, since institutional access is not yet in place. The vignettes below sketch what each team is doing and which frontier capability matters most to their work.

Reespire

Tavishi Chaturvedi MSBA '26 et al. · climate & data-driven environmental tech

Reespire sits at the intersection of climate solutions and data-intensive analysis. For a team led by an MSBA student, the load-bearing capability is long-context synthesis — the ability to pull a coherent argument out of hundreds of pages of environmental regulation, patent filings, or academic literature in a single pass.

What matters most
Long-context reasoning (Claude Opus 4.7 and Gemini 3.1 Pro both ship one-million-token context windows in 2026).
What changes with access
Research synthesis that previously took weeks of reading happens in an afternoon — and is reproducible the next time the data updates.

Finexus Tech

Gabrielle Lopes '28 · fintech

Fintech ventures live or die on precision. The reasoning quality and "literal adherence" of Anthropic's Opus-class models — the work it does not get creative about — is the trait that matters in regulated, brand-sensitive contexts where erratic outputs are a liability rather than a feature.

What matters most
Careful, literal reasoning under regulatory constraints (Claude Opus 4.7's strength).
What changes with access
An undergraduate-led venture can prototype algorithmic-implementation work that previously required a hired senior engineer.

BobbyBrowser

Krish Bhatia '26, Aryan Patil · browser-automation

BobbyBrowser is exactly the kind of product the new agentic tools were built for. OpenAI's Operator — the underlying "Computer-Using Agent" inside Workspace Agents — navigates web pages, fills forms, and runs multi-step procurement workflows. For a small team, it is the difference between needing engineers and not.

What matters most
Agentic browser automation and tool use (OpenAI's Operator / Workspace Agents).
What changes with access
A two-person team ships what previously required a six-person engineering org.

Sketos

Santiago Ortega Ramirez MBA '26 · multimodal business analysis

Sketos's work draws on visual data — charts, infographics, decks. Google Gemini's native multimodality (the only frontier model that ingests video alongside text, image, and audio in a single pass) is the unlock. Combined with Workspace integration, an MBA-led team can generate grounded, editable sales materials directly from proprietary data.

What matters most
Native multimodal input + Workspace integration (Gemini 3.1 Pro).
What changes with access
Sales-material production that previously took a design contractor becomes a same-day task inside the team.

The number of Babson graduates starting companies is a number we want to grow. These tools are how.

Already in classrooms

Babson + Founderz: the literacy layer is in place.

Through "AI in Action" with Founderz, Babson has already begun bringing frontier-AI fluency into the curriculum, with the AI-THINK methodology providing a structured framework for applying these tools in entrepreneurial settings. That work is the literacy layer. The remaining move — direct partnership with the frontier labs themselves — is the tooling layer underneath it.

One without the other works less well than the two together.
