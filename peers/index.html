In practice

What a mature frontier-AI partnership looks like, four ways.

Four peer institutions have each taken a different path to a frontier-AI partnership. The variety is the point — there is no single template. The shared signal is that the most active schools have made formal institutional commitments.

Four examples

Different shapes, same direction.

The Wharton School

Strategic AI investment · MBA major in AI for Business · OpenAI

In May 2024 Wharton announced a strategic investment in AI research and teaching, providing AI access to all full-time and executive MBA students and establishing the AI & Analytics for Business program1. In April 2025 Wharton launched a new MBA major in Artificial Intelligence for Business alongside an undergraduate concentration2.

Shape
Institutional AI access for the MBA population + a new dedicated degree program.
What it signals
Wharton treats AI as core curriculum, not as an electives-track topic.

Northeastern University

Campus-wide Anthropic agreement · 13 global campuses

In April 2025 Northeastern announced a campus-wide agreement with Anthropic, providing access to Claude for all students, faculty, and staff across 13 global campuses3. The partnership emphasizes Anthropic's "Learning Mode" — designed to guide student reasoning rather than just produce answers4.

Shape
Single-lab institutional partnership at large scale.
What it signals
"Learning Mode" pedagogy is the differentiator; Anthropic is the lab universities often pick when teaching is central to the use case.

Stanford University

AIMES · $1M seed grants · faculty-driven pedagogy R&D

Stanford has invested in the curriculum layer directly. AIMES (AI Meets Education at Stanford) is a $1 million seed-grant initiative funding faculty–student projects to test new approaches to AI in teaching5. The framing emphasizes deepening learning rather than short-circuiting it.

Shape
Internal grant-funded pedagogy R&D, vendor-agnostic.
What it signals
Tool access alone is not enough; the institution needs to invest in figuring out what changes in the classroom.

California State University System

~460,000 students · 63,000 faculty & staff · ChatGPT Edu

In February 2025 the CSU system announced a landmark initiative — what it described as the nation's largest AI-empowered university system — providing ChatGPT Edu access to more than 460,000 students and over 63,000 faculty and staff6. The deployment uses a "walled garden" architecture where student data does not train underlying models.

Shape
System-wide deployment with data-isolation guarantees.
What it signals
When access is universal, the experimentation surface widens dramatically.
What this shows

The field has decided this is institutional infrastructure.

Four schools, four different shapes — universal MBA seats, single-lab campus deals, faculty grant programs, system-wide rollouts. The path forward is not standardized. The fact that there is a path forward, and that peers are walking it on different timelines, is what's worth noting.
