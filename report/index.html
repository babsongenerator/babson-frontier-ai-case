Section 1

The moment

A short reference for getting up to speed on the three AI providers whose tools dominate higher-education deployments in 2026.

OpenAI, Anthropic, and Google lead AI in 2026. Their education-tier products are mature, peer institutions are already deploying them at scale, and the per-user cost is now squarely inside ordinary software-budget territory. The pages that follow are the evidence — what each provider offers, what each costs, and which peers have already moved.

The three providers

Three companies, three distinct strengths.

The 2026 AI landscape is, in practice, the work of three companies. Each ships enterprise-grade education tools that go far beyond chat — and each is differentiated in useful ways. A relationship with all three — common at peer institutions — does not require choosing a winner; it puts whichever provider best fits the moment into students' hands.

OpenAI is the most widely-deployed and the strongest on agentic execution. GPT-5.5, released in April 20261, leads on multi-step workflow automation. Workspace Agents — launched the same week — run agents inside Salesforce, Slack, and HubSpot on the user's behalf2.

Anthropic is widely treated as the "architect" of the three — careful, literal, well-suited to high-stakes reasoning. Claude Opus 4.7 is the current flagship3. Claude for Education ships a Learning Mode designed to guide student reasoning rather than just produce answers — pedagogy first4.

Google brings native multimodality and a one-million-token context window with Gemini 3.1 Pro5, plus deep integration into the Workspace tools many students already use daily — Workspace Intelligence launched in April 20266. Gemini for Education already reaches more than 10 million students across 1,000+ institutions7.

Peer momentum

The institutions Babson watches have already moved.

Different shapes, same direction. Each of the three providers has at least one elite peer institution deploying it at scale.

OpenAI · 2024–25

Wharton, Oxford, UCLA, CSU.

Wharton put ChatGPT in every MBA student's hands8; Oxford rolled out to all staff and students9; CSU reached 460,000+ students10.

Anthropic · 2025

Northeastern, LSE, Champlain.

Northeastern signed campus-wide across 13 campuses11; LSE made enterprise Claude free for all students; Champlain — a small private peer — got two years of free Claude Pro12.

Google · 2025–26

Rice, Houston, 1,000+ others.

Rice deployed Gemini campus-wide13; Houston rolled out with data sovereignty14; Google's published footprint reaches 10M+ U.S. students7.

Three things to note

Three things that are true of all three providers today.

Mature: each provider offers an institution-grade product with SSO, Workspace/Office integration, and data-isolation guarantees standard.

Priced: per-user rates now sit well inside what institutions spend on routine software. ChatGPT Business is $20/user/month15; Anthropic and Google offer comparable education-tier pricing through institutional channels.

Deployed: already in peer institutions — 1,000+ on Gemini, campus-wide Anthropic deals at Northeastern, LSE, and Champlain, and ChatGPT Edu at Oxford and CSU. Section 4 details each.

Section 2

Value for students, faculty, staff

Institutional access to OpenAI, Anthropic, and Google benefits students, faculty, and staff in different — and complementary — ways. The case below is built from the documented capabilities of ChatGPT, Claude, and Gemini today.

For students

The compression effect, applied to everything.

The traits these tools reward — fast iteration, comfort with ambiguity, willingness to ship — map directly to the work student entrepreneurs do.

Build velocity

Ship a prototype in a weekend.

OpenAI's Workspace Agents and the underlying Operator engine2 let students run multi-step workflows inside enterprise tools without code. A two-person team can launch what previously required a small engineering org.

Research depth

Read hundreds of pages in one pass.

Claude Opus 4.73 and Gemini 3.1 Pro5 both ship one-million-token context windows. Market research, regulatory analysis, and patent landscape sweeps become tractable in an afternoon.

Better data

Collect, clean, analyze at scale.

Agents can pull data from web sources, normalize it, and produce structured outputs that feed business plans, customer-discovery interviews, and competitive analysis.

Multimodal work

Video, image, audio — natively.

Gemini 3.1 Pro is currently the only AI model that handles video alongside text, image, and audio in a single pass5. For product, design, and content ventures, that collapses workflows that used to need specialized tools.

Personal tutor

An always-on, pedagogically-aware mentor.

Anthropic's Learning Mode, included in Claude for Education4, guides reasoning through questions rather than handing back answers — closer to how good faculty already teach.

Job-market signal

Skills employers already expect.

Fluency with ChatGPT, Claude, and Gemini is increasingly a baseline employer expectation. A graduate who has spent two years using all three providers as part of coursework arrives signal-strong.

For faculty

Teaching leverage, research leverage.

Faculty gain on two axes: classroom productivity and research capacity. ChatGPT, Claude, and Gemini each supply distinct, complementary tools for both.

Course development

Build and update materials faster.

Draft new case-study syllabi, generate problem sets, refresh examples each semester, and personalize materials by section — in a fraction of the previous time.

Office hours augmentation

A class-specific assistant, 24/7.

Claude's Learning Mode4 and similar features from the other providers let faculty stand up a course-specific helper that guides student reasoning between office hours.

Research depth

Synthesize a literature in a sitting.

One-million-token context windows35 let a researcher pull together a literature review or methods summary across hundreds of papers in one pass.

Grading consistency

Apply rubrics with calibrated consistency.

AI-assisted rubric application improves consistency across sections and semesters — useful in large enrollment courses where calibration drift is a real concern. Final assessment still sits with the instructor.

Cross-departmental work

Collaborate across the institution.

Google Workspace Intelligence6 weaves Gemini directly into the Workspace tools Babson already uses, lowering the friction of cross-departmental research and curriculum projects.

Less admin

Less time on the non-teaching parts.

Drafting committee summaries, scheduling, syllabus updates, recommendation letters — much of the administrative tax on faculty time compresses dramatically.

For staff

Workflow automation across the institution.

Staff in advising, admissions, the Blank Center, alumni relations, finance, HR, and IT each gain in measurable ways — the same compression that benefits student founders applies to every recurring institutional workflow.

Workflow automation

Multi-step processes run themselves.

OpenAI's Workspace Agents2 automate multi-system workflows — pulling data from Salesforce, drafting follow-ups in email, updating Slack — at human-quality output.

Data collection

Better data, less effort.

Agents can collect, clean, and analyze data across institutional systems and external sources — supporting better reporting, advising, and decision-making across departments.

Document processing

Read and route at scale.

Long-context models35 ingest contracts, policy documents, board materials, and accreditation reports in one pass — accelerating review cycles that historically take weeks.

Reporting & analytics

Reports on demand.

A staff member with provider access can produce ad-hoc reports, dashboards, and analyses without waiting on a centralized analytics team.

Communications

Draft and personalize at scale.

Donor communications, student outreach, alumni updates — all benefit from draft-quality first passes that staff then edit. Volume up; consistency improves; time per piece falls.

IT & helpdesk

Tier-1 support that actually helps.

Provider tools can power internal helpdesks that resolve common requests immediately. Walled-garden deployments10 keep institutional data isolated from public training.

The compounding effect

Each of the three groups gains independently. The bigger effect is what happens when they gain together. Students arrive in classrooms taught by faculty using these tools, run their ventures with staff who deploy AI fluently, and graduate having used all three providers daily for years. That compounding shows up only when students, faculty, and staff are all working inside the same set of tools.

Where institutions provision all three providers, the documented gains accrue to every campus group simultaneously — in patterns that aren't available when any one is missing.

AI does not replace the entrepreneur. It compresses the time between an idea and the first version a customer can hold.

The four ventures below — drawn from the 2025 Summer Venture Program16 — show four different kinds of work, and the three providers students reach for to do them. The capability mapping is illustrative; SVP itself does not prescribe a tool stack.

Summer Venture Program 2025

Four teams, four kinds of AI work.

Liaizon

Anushka Balaraj '25 · AI-powered platform

An explicitly AI-native venture, where the three providers are the infrastructure. For founders building on top of these models, the question is not which provider to use but which fits which task — and how the choice shifts as capabilities advance quarter to quarter. Anthropic's Opus 4.7 (April 20263), OpenAI's GPT-5.5 (April 20261), and Google's Gemini 3.1 Pro5 are the three options in 2026.

Pipelign Software

Greg Van Aken MBA '25 · business software

Software-for-business ventures benefit most from agentic execution. OpenAI's Workspace Agents, launched in April 20262, run multi-step workflows directly inside enterprise tools (Salesforce, Slack, HubSpot). For a small team, this is the difference between needing a sales-ops engineer and not.

GENETICFIT Labs

Alexander Sica '27 · personalized fitness from genetic data

Data-intensive ventures lean on long-context synthesis. Both Anthropic's Claude Opus 4.73 and Google's Gemini 3.1 Pro5 ship one-million-token context windows in 2026, which makes it tractable for a small team to integrate regulatory, scientific, and customer data in a single pass.

Christelle Reppen MBA '26, Justin Demers · creative product development

Visual and multimodal ventures benefit most from Google's Gemini, currently the only AI model that handles video alongside text, image, and audio in a single pass5. Workspace Intelligence integrates that capability directly into the productivity tools students already use6.

Four kinds of work. Three providers — OpenAI, Anthropic, Google — that students reach for to do them.

Section 4

The three providers

Two questions decide this: what does each provider cost, and which peer institutions have already deployed it at scale? Both are answered below — alongside what each is, in 2026.

OpenAI · ChatGPT for institutions

OpenAI is the most widely-deployed of the three providers in higher education. Its flagship offerings for institutions are ChatGPT Business (commercial) and ChatGPT Edu (academic), built on the GPT-5.5 model family1. The April 2026 launch of Workspace Agents added native agentic execution inside enterprise tools like Salesforce, Slack, and HubSpot2.

What it costs

PlanPer user / monthNotes
ChatGPT Business$20.00 (committed)Price reduced by $5 in April 202615.
ChatGPT Enterprise / EduNegotiatedVolume pricing for institutional deployments; standard for systems serving 1,000+ students.

How peers have leveraged it

The Wharton School

Universal MBA access · new AI for Business major

In May 2024 Wharton announced a strategic investment in AI, providing access to all full-time and executive MBA students and establishing the AI & Analytics for Business program8. In April 2025 Wharton launched a new MBA major in Artificial Intelligence for Business alongside an undergraduate concentration17.

University of Oxford

First UK university with ChatGPT Edu for all · 5-year OpenAI partnership

In September 2025 Oxford became the first UK university to offer ChatGPT Edu to all staff and students, following an initial year of access for ~750 staff and researchers. The rollout is part of a five-year partnership with OpenAI that includes research grant funding and enterprise security9.

UCLA

First California university with ChatGPT Enterprise

UCLA introduced ChatGPT Enterprise in September 2024 — the first California university to do so. Access was extended to students, faculty, and staff engaged in teaching, learning, and research initiatives, with an open call for project submissions18.

California State University System

~460,000 students · 63,000 faculty & staff · ChatGPT Edu

In February 2025 the CSU system announced what it described as the nation's largest AI-empowered university system — providing ChatGPT Edu access to more than 460,000 students and over 63,000 faculty and staff. The deployment uses a "walled garden" architecture where student data does not train the underlying model10.

Anthropic · Claude for institutions

Anthropic is widely treated as the "architect" of the three providers — careful, literal, and designed around safety. Claude Opus 4.7 is the current flagship model3. Claude for Education ships a Learning Mode designed to guide student reasoning rather than just produce answers — pedagogy first4.

What it costs

PlanPer user / monthNotes
Claude for EducationNegotiatedInstitutional contracts; Learning Mode included4. Some launch partners received multi-year free access (see Champlain, below).
Claude EnterpriseNegotiatedStandard enterprise contract for non-education use cases.

How peers have leveraged it

Northeastern University

Campus-wide Claude · 13 global campuses · April 2025

Northeastern signed a campus-wide agreement with Anthropic in April 2025, providing Claude access to all students, faculty, and staff across 13 global campuses11. The partnership foregrounds Learning Mode pedagogy4.

London School of Economics

Full campus Claude access · launch partner

LSE was named as an Anthropic launch partner for Claude for Education in April 2025, with Enterprise-tier Claude (with extended context) made available free to every LSE student and educator under a 12-month launch agreement. Student access opened in May 202519.

Champlain College

~2,000 undergrads · Vermont · 2-year free Claude Pro

Champlain — a small, private, career-focused undergraduate college — received campus-wide Claude Pro access for all students, faculty, and staff (on-campus and online), free for two years through June 2027. Joint classroom and research projects span digital forensics, cybersecurity, business entrepreneurship, and humanities12. The closest small-private peer to Babson in Anthropic's launch cohort.

Google · Gemini for institutions

Google brings native multimodality (the only major AI model that handles video alongside text, image, and audio in a single pass) and a one-million-token context window, with Gemini 3.1 Pro as the current flagship5. Most universities deploy Gemini through Gemini for Education or Workspace Intelligence, which weaves Gemini into the Workspace tools many institutions already use6.

What it costs

PlanPer user / monthNotes
Google AI Pro for EducationEducation tier · via resellerEducation-specific tier for Workspace for Education customers; pricing handled through Google's reseller channel20.
Workspace IntelligenceBundled with Workspace plansAdd-on for existing Workspace tenants; institutional pricing6.

How peers have leveraged it

Rice University

Campus-wide Gemini + NotebookLM · September 2025

Rice — a private research university with a strong business school — adopted Google's Gemini for Education and NotebookLM campus-wide for all students, faculty, and staff in September 2025, with the partnership grounded in "responsible AI"13.

University of Houston

System-wide Gemini for Education · full data sovereignty

The University of Houston announced a first-in-state systemwide deployment of Gemini for Education and NotebookLM in March 2026 — all students, faculty, and staff — with explicit data sovereignty: institutional data and research IP are not used to train public models14.

1,000+ institutions, 10M+ students

Google's published higher-ed footprint

Google's own roundup confirms Gemini for Education is in use at more than 1,000 higher-education institutions reaching over 10 million U.S. college students, including Brown, MIT, Ohio State, Michigan, Notre Dame, Florida State, UC Riverside, Texas State, Rice, and the California Community Colleges7.

Putting it together

What this looks like, side by side.

Each provider has a distinct shape. OpenAI — the most widely-adopted, strongest on agentic execution — is at Wharton, Oxford, UCLA, and the CSU system. Anthropic — pedagogy-first, Learning Mode at its core — is at Northeastern, LSE, and Champlain. Google — multimodal and woven into Workspace — is at Rice, Houston, and a thousand other institutions reaching 10 million students.

All three are available now. Per-user pricing sits inside what institutions spend on routine software. Education tiers exist for exactly this kind of rollout. Peer institutions across the U.S. and U.K. have already deployed each at scale.
