3 of 3 · The ask

As the #1 school for entrepreneurship, Babson can and should negotiate college-wide pricing with frontier model providers.

Until recently, Babson supported Google products. Restoring that relationship would provide students access to Gemini.

For an institution like Babson, partnering with the three providers for a 5,000-user deployment involves transitioning from retail “Pro” subscriptions to negotiated Enterprise Service Agreements (ESAs). These agreements prioritize data privacy (FERPA compliance) and volume-based discounts that can reduce per-user costs by 50% to 70% compared to individual plans.

The economic landscape at 5,000 seats

Provider Partnership model Estimated annual cost Strategic leverage
OpenAI $200k – $300k Best-in-class reasoning; dedicated university privacy tier.
Google Gemini Education $600k – $900k * Seamless integration into existing Babson Google Workspace.
Anthropic Claude Enterprise $700k – $850k Superior for long-form research (500k+ context windows).

* Google often offers Student Advantage multipliers, where purchasing 1,000 faculty seats unlocks 4,000 student seats at no extra cost — effectively lowering the average cost to roughly $3–$5 per user per month.

How peers have negotiated

Large-scale AI deployments in 2025–2026 show that universities rarely pay sticker price. Three patterns recur:

The student multiplier · Arizona State University

ASU was the first major institution to sign a comprehensive partnership with OpenAI. Institutions with 5,000+ users typically secure rates of $40–$60 per user per year for — compared with the $240/year a consumer pays for ChatGPT Plus.

Bundle discounts · University of Michigan

U-M built “U-M GPT,” a platform that abstracts Gemini, GPT-4, and Claude into one interface. By licensing via API volume commitments rather than individual seats, they avoided the rigid per-user cost structure. Minimum-spend buckets can be 30–50% cheaper than equivalent per-seat pricing.

Pilot-to-scale credits · Wharton / UPenn

Institutions with an active innovation-lab footprint — the parallel for Babson is — can leverage Proof-of-Concept credits: Anthropic and Google frequently provide 6–12 months of free high-tier access for a subset of users, in exchange for a commitment to a full-campus rollout the following year.

A scale example

California State University’s deal with OpenAI was $17 million over 18 months for more than 500,000 users — the largest publicly-disclosed peer-institution AI commitment to date. Single-school deals at Wharton, Oxford, Northeastern, and others have not been disclosed in dollars. Education-tier discounts and volume pricing typically reduce list figures by 30 to 60 percent.

A strategic recommendation

For a “sovereign stack” philosophy, the most cost-effective path is likely a hybrid tier:

  1. Campus-wide: Google Gemini Education, bundled with the existing Babson Google Workspace footprint.
  2. Specialized: A ChatGPT Team or Claude Enterprise license for the ~500 power users in or Moonshot Strategy courses, where advanced reasoning and long context windows matter most.
