Benchmarking with peer business schools

Of the fifteen peer institutions that have committed to one of the three frontier AI providers in the last twenty-four months, most include the business school directly in the deployment. Wharton bought universal MBA seats from OpenAI outright, and Tuck followed as the first business school to sign a ChatGPT Edu agreement covering all students, faculty, and professional staff. Institution-wide deals at Oxford (Saïd), UCLA (Anderson), the California State University system, Arizona State (W. P. Carey), Duke (Fuqua), Vanderbilt (Owen), Northeastern (D’Amore-McKim), the London School of Economics, Champlain, Columbia (Columbia Business School), Rice (Jones), and the University of Houston (Bauer) all cover the business school by default. (Harvard’s Faculty of Arts and Sciences is a separate FAS-only Claude rollout; Harvard Business School takes a different approach described below.) None of these is a pilot.

How the business schools are served

  • Wharton bought universal MBA seats from OpenAI.
  • Tuck signed the first business-school ChatGPT Edu agreement — covering all students, faculty, and professional staff — and now also has campus access to Microsoft Copilot and Anthropic.
  • Duke (Fuqua), Vanderbilt (Owen), and Rice (Jones) each sit inside their universities’ campus-wide AI deployments.
  • The California State University system’s $17M deal covers business programs across all 23 campuses.

Harvard Business School provides every major model

Through direct licenses and Harvard’s own AI Sandbox, HBS MBA students reach OpenAI, Anthropic, and Google models from inside a single school. HBS has provided ChatGPT to all MBA students since fall 2023, and the 2026 toolkit adds Claude, Claude Code, and a handful of secondary tools (Lovable, Julius AI, Manus, Gamma). The approach is “support every tool the students need” — closer to what Babson students are already doing on their own.

Babson has the scale, the reputation, and the institutional discretion to choose the partnership that fits.
