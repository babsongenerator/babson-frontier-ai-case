Benchmarking with peer business schools

Of the fifteen peer institutions that have committed to one of the three frontier AI providers in the last twenty-four months, most include the business school directly in the deployment. Wharton bought universal MBA seats from OpenAI outright, and Tuck followed as the first business school to sign a ChatGPT Edu agreement covering all students, faculty, and professional staff. Institution-wide deals at Oxford (Saïd), UCLA (Anderson), the California State University system, Arizona State (W. P. Carey), Duke (Fuqua), Vanderbilt (Owen), Northeastern (D’Amore-McKim), the London School of Economics, Champlain, Columbia (Columbia Business School), Rice (Jones), and the University of Houston (Bauer) all cover the business school by default. (Harvard’s Faculty of Arts and Sciences is a separate FAS-only Claude rollout; Harvard Business School takes a different approach described below.) None of these is a pilot.
